<Chapter title="The Awakening" number={1} />

The air felt unusually heavy, thick with the scent of damp earth and ozone. I opened my eyes, but the darkness refused to yield.

<Thought>
  Am I still dreaming? 
</Thought>

<CinematicImage src="/images/stories/the-girl-in-the-dream/the-girl-in-the-dream-banner.png" alt="A dark, misty forest path" />

A distant hum resonated through the floorboards. It wasn't a sound, not really—it was a vibration that I felt in my teeth.

<Dialogue speaker="Unknown Voice">
  You shouldn't be awake yet. The cycle hasn't completed.
</Dialogue>

<Dialogue speaker="Me">
  Where am I? What cycle?
</Dialogue>

The silence that followed stretched out, unnerving in its completeness. Then, a rustle of paper. I reached out in the dark and my fingers brushed against something coarse.

<Letter>
  If you are reading this, the protocol has failed. 
  Do not trust the voices. 
  Do not follow the lights.
  
  — E.
</Letter>

<Pause />

I folded the paper carefully, slipping it into my pocket. The darkness was no longer just an absence of light; it felt alive, watching me. Waiting.

<WideImage src="/images/stories/the-girl-in-the-dream/the-girl-in-the-dream-banner.png" alt="An abstract sweeping landscape" />

This was where the dream ended. And the nightmare began.
